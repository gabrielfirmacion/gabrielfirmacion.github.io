Asking Good Questions

If you ask well, you'll get the right answer

August 14, 2015

There are a lot of times that you will get stuck writing code or the code that you are trying to write will not cooperate with what you had in mind. After spending a lot of time doing some research and trying to figure out what is wrong, you still cannot solve it by yourself. Your best solution to figure out your problem is asking a question to your peers or online.

Asking questions is a great way to solve your problems but in order to achieve getting the right answer you needed, you should know how to ask good and proper questions. Though it sounds simple, it is actually a tricky thing because if you do not know how to formulate good questions, people will just answer your questions based on how they understood it and you may receive multiple different answers as a result. In my blog post today, I will teach you some simple pointers on how to formulate a good question.

First of all, just go ahead and type your question. Is your question easy to understand? Is it clear? Does it contain all the information needed in order for people to answer your question correctly? If you can answer 'yes' to all these questions then you are headed in the right direction in asking a good question.

Next, your question should be concrete and specific. If I asked you "how do you write an if/esle statement?" There will most probably be a lot of follow up questions like "for what language?" and "What are you trying to return?" So I would probably write, "What is the correct syntax in writing an if/else statement for Ruby that returns true and false if otherwise?"

The most important thing in writing your question is it should be clear to the readers of your question what you are trying to achieve with your code so that they will know how to help you out. If think your question is too complex, try adding the code you want help on so that the readers will see what you are trying to achieve and if you already know the return values that you want to get, add those as well.

Last but not least, review your question. Check for wrong spelling and punctuations. One missing punctuation can possibly confuse your readers already so make sure to double check.

There you have it. If you follow these guidelines in asking your questions, then you should be fine! If you want to ask me anything, feel free to send me an email and make sure to ask good questions :)
